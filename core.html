The System

Concrete + Glass. Cast on Site. Finished by the Owner.

BuilderK CORE is a different way to build a home. Instead of CMU block and stick framing, the entire structure — footings, slab, walls, and ceiling — is cast in one continuous pour using reusable aluminum forms. The result is a solid, monolithic concrete home delivered at roughly $85/sqft, a fraction of the $150/sqft Florida standard.

The product is monolithic cast-in-place concrete using aluminum forms, poured around lightweight aerated concrete to reduce weight and improve insulation. The buyer gets a sealed, livable concrete shell with stucco exterior, sealed concrete floors, and basic MEP. They finish the rest themselves over time — at their own pace, with their own taste.

Concrete + big glass is the aesthetic that Millennials and Gen Z fall in love with. We add high-performance hurricane glass to maximize that aesthetic and produce homes that feel premium even at workforce-housing prices. It is a textbook unlock — and the system already works at scale in Mexico, India, and across Latin America.

The aluminum-form cast-on-site concrete system is not a science experiment. BuilderK's leadership personally delivered 1,500 homes using this exact approach two decades ago. Where CORE wins biggest

The Footings.

Traditional FL construction: footings = 2 days of trench + form + rebar, then 3 days of cure before you can do anything else. That's 5 of your 10 days gone before a single wall goes up.

CORE: footings are cast monolithically with the slab, walls, and ceiling in one pour. Aluminum forms surround the entire structure including the thickened footing edges. One pour day for everything.

Result: 5 days saved per home on the footing step alone. The video above shows exactly how the footings integrate with the wall forms — watch it.

Brazil's MRV is the world's largest deployer of this exact aluminum-form single-pour system — over 200,000 homes built. Walls + slabs + footings in one shot, mass-housing scale, low-cost.

The Concrete

Lightweight Cellular Concrete

Lighter. Better insulation. Same fire + hurricane resistance.

Lightweight aerated concrete blocks (Ytong) — France 2012. Same material poured into aluminum forms.

Cellular Lightweight Concrete (CLC)

Weight20–60% lighter than normal concrete
Density80–110 pcf (vs 150 normal)
Strength1,500–3,000 PSI (sufficient for 1–2 story)
R-valueR-2.5 to R-4 per inch — built in
6" wall R-valueR-15 to R-24 (CMU = R-2)
Fire rating4-hour
Wind rating200+ mph (Cat-5)

Air entrainment + foaming agents create millions of tiny air bubbles in the concrete. The result: concrete that floats on water in some mixes — yet still hurricane-proof and fire-rated. Florida's leading AAC supplier is in Haines City — 50 minutes from BuilderK HQ.

Footings 101

The Two Branches of Residential Footings

Every residential foundation in Florida is one of two types: Traditional CMU spread footing (the standard) or Monolithic Concrete footing (the CORE approach). Below is the full side-by-side.

Branch A · Legacy Florida

Traditional CMU Spread Footing

Dig trench → place rebar → form perimeter → pour footing → wait 3 days to cure → strip forms → build CMU stem wall course-by-course → slab pour on top.

Time5–7 days
Crews needed3 (excavation, rebar/forms, masons)
Inspections3 (rebar, footing pour, stem wall)
Concrete pours3 (footing, stem-wall grout, slab)
Cure delays3 days minimum
⚡ Upgrade for traditional builds

Fab-Form FastFoot® — fabric formwork for residential footings. Replaces plywood + 2×4 forms with a single fabric form that's faster, cheaper, and zero waste. Cuts traditional footing time by 30–50%.

The verdict: Even with FastFoot improvements, traditional CMU footings still need a separate pour day + cure cycle. 5 days of your 10-day window — gone before walls go up.
★ Branch B · CORE method

Monolithic Concrete Footing

Excavate + place rebar inside aluminum forms → forms encapsulate footings + slab + walls + ceiling as a single rigid assembly → ONE concrete pour → strip next day → start next home with same forms.

Time1 day
Crews needed1 (form-set crew, all stages)
Inspections1 (combined)
Concrete pours1 (everything)
Cure delays12–24 hours total

🔑 The 4 things to notice when watching

  1. The forms interlock like LEGO. No welding, no custom fabrication on site. Just clip together panels.
  2. Walls, slab, and ceiling are all formed simultaneously. Not poured separately — the entire structure is one continuous concrete shell.
  3. One pour day fills everything. The concrete pump truck connects to the form and fills the entire assembly through the top.
  4. Forms strip in hours. 12–24 hours after pour, the same forms come off and immediately move to the next home.
