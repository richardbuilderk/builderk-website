Control the cost

Budgets & cost control

Every project runs on a budget built from the estimate and coded to the WBS / cost codes — so estimates, POs, invoices and change orders all roll up the same way and we can compare jobs.

  • Budget loaded in app.builderk.com, coded to the WBS.
  • Schedule of values aligned to the cost codes (and to the draw schedule).
  • Budget vs. actual tracked continuously as POs and invoices post.
  • Job cost variance is a tracked KPI — target ≤ 5%, cost review at > 7% (see Operations → KPIs).
  • Change orders re-baseline the budget so variance stays honest.
Fund the work

Draws & lender

On a financed project, money is released by the lender in draws tied to completed work. The draw schedule is mapped to milestones at setup (see Pre-Construction → Finance).

The draw request

StepWhat happens
1 · Measure% complete / milestone reached, per the schedule of values
2 · InspectLender / inspector verifies the work in place
3 · WaiversCollect lien waivers for the work being paid
4 · SubmitDraw request package to the lender
5 · FundLender funds; subs & suppliers paid
💵 Retainage
A retainage % is held from each sub payment and released at closeout once punch is cleared and final waivers are in.
Protect the project

Compliance & insurance

Lien waivers

  • Conditional waivers as progress payments are made; unconditional on final payment.
  • Collected from every sub and supplier — this is what protects the client's clear title (see Closeout → financials).

Insurance

  • Builder's Risk — per project, active before work starts.
  • BuilderK — General Liability + Workers' Comp.
  • Subcontractor COIs — GL + WC, BuilderK named as certificate holder (gate in the checklist).

Filings

  • Notice of Commencement (NOC) filed & posted before work (see Site setup).
