The finish line

Substantial completion & CO

Once every final inspection passes, the building final is issued and the jurisdiction grants the Certificate of Occupancy (CO) — the legal green light that the home can be occupied. Substantial completion is defined in the client's contract; the CO is the objective anchor.

Site demobilization

  • Final clean — interior & exterior, windows, fixtures.
  • Remove temporary power/water, fencing, dumpster, portable toilet and the job sign.
  • Final grade, sod & driveway complete; site left clean.
The moment

Client orientation & walkthrough

The goal is a zero-defect handover: our own quality walk closes the punch before the client ever sees it, so the orientation is about handing over a finished home — not making a list.

  • Our pre-handover quality walk — finishes against the NAHB guidelines, function-test everything.
  • Client orientation walk — how the home works: shutoffs, panels, HVAC, appliances, finishes & maintenance.
  • Punch sign-off — any remaining items logged, owned and dated; client signs acceptance.
  • Keys & access handed over with the owner package.
What the owner gets

Owner handover package

  • Certificate of Occupancy copy
  • As-built survey & any as-built plan markups
  • Product & appliance manuals + manufacturer warranties
  • Paint, finish & selections schedule (colors, models, suppliers)
  • Subcontractor & supplier list for warranty routing
  • Final lien releases & final affidavit
  • Maintenance guide + shutoff / emergency reference
  • Warranty document with start date & how to file a claim
Closing the books

Closeout financials

  • Final lender draw submitted with CO & final lien waivers.
  • Lien waivers / releases collected from every sub & supplier (conditional → unconditional on final payment).
  • Retainage released once punch is cleared and waivers are in.
  • Final client invoice issued & paid; deposit reconciled.
  • Cost codes closed — final budget vs. actual captured for the next estimate.
🔒 No final payment without waivers
Final retainage and final sub payments release only against signed lien waivers — that's what protects the client's clear title.
After we hand over the keys

Warranty period & claims

The warranty we provide is defined in the client's contract — typically a workmanship warranty (commonly one year) covering defects in materials and workmanship, alongside manufacturer warranties on products. The standard for what's a covered defect is the NAHB performance guidelines.

⚖️ Florida timeframes (general — confirm with counsel)
Florida construction-defect claims run on a 4-year statute of limitations and a 10-year statute of repose (§95.11). This is background, not legal advice — warranty terms are set by the contract.

The claim process

  • Client submits a claim (form / portal) with description & photos.
  • Triage — emergency (life-safety, active water/electrical) gets an immediate response; routine items are scheduled.
  • Assess against the NAHB guidelines; route product issues to the manufacturer, workmanship to the responsible sub.
  • Resolve, verify with the client, and log it for the record.

Closeout checklist

All of this is done before we call a project closed.
  • CO obtained
  • All finals passed
  • Final clean + site demobilized
  • Punch closed & signed
  • Owner package delivered
  • Keys handed over
  • Lien waivers collected (all subs)
  • Retainage released
  • Final invoice paid & reconciled
  • Budget vs. actual captured
  • Warranty start logged
  • Project archived in BuilderK
