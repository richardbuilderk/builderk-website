The standard

OSHA 29 CFR 1926 — Construction

OSHA's construction standards (29 CFR 1926) set the federal floor for jobsite safety. As the general contractor, BuilderK carries duties on a multi-employer worksite — our controls protect our own people and everyone working the site.

⛑️ The Focus Four — what kills on construction sites
Most construction fatalities come from four hazards. Every site control we run targets these first.
Falls

From height — roofs, edges, openings, ladders, scaffolds.

Struck-by

Vehicles, equipment, falling/flying objects.

Caught-in / between

Trenches, machinery, collapsing material.

Electrocution

Power lines, energized circuits, tools.

Key subparts we live in

  • Subpart M — Fall protection (required at 6 ft in construction)
  • Subpart L — Scaffolds · Subpart X — Stairways & ladders
  • Subpart P — Excavations (protective systems, competent person)
  • Subpart K — Electrical (GFCI, lockout/tagout, overhead lines)
  • Subpart E — Personal protective equipment
  • Subpart CC — Cranes & derricks · silica & HazCom (health)
  • Subpart C — general safety & the competent person requirement

Training

Crews should hold OSHA 10-hour (workers) or 30-hour (supervisors) outreach cards. Task-specific competent-person training is required for excavations, scaffolds and fall protection.

How we run it

Our site safety program

  • A designated competent person / site supervisor on every active site, with authority to stop work.
  • Site-specific orientation before anyone — staff or sub — starts work.
  • Daily walk for hazards; correct or barricade immediately.
  • Housekeeping, access control and signage kept current.
  • First-aid kit, eyewash, fire extinguisher on site and stocked.
  • Emergency plan posted — nearest hospital, muster point, site address for 911.
Weekly habit

Toolbox talks

A short (10–15 min) safety talk before work, tied to the week's scope and hazards.

  • Weekly, led by the site supervisor.
  • Topic matches the work — e.g. trench week → excavation & cave-in.
  • Documented with a sign-in sheet kept in the site file.
  • Pull from the toolbox-talk library (coming to this page).

When something happens

Incident & near-miss reporting

Report every injury, incident and near-miss to the site supervisor immediately. Near-misses matter — they tell us where the next injury is coming from, so we fix the hazard before it lands.

🚨 OSHA reporting clock — don't miss it
  • Fatality → report within 8 hours.
  • In-patient hospitalization, amputation, or loss of an eye → within 24 hours.
Report at 1-800-321-OSHA (6742) or osha.gov. The site supervisor calls leadership first.

Recordkeeping

  • Classify: first-aid (log internally) vs recordable vs lost-time.
  • Covered employers keep the OSHA 300 log and post the 300A summary Feb 1–Apr 30.
  • Every incident gets a short root-cause and a corrective action.
On the wall

Required postings

  • OSHA "Job Safety & Health: It's the Law" poster (OSHA 3165) — required on every site.
  • Emergency numbers + nearest hospital/urgent care + the site's 911 address.
  • Safety Data Sheets (SDS) accessible for every chemical on site.
  • OSHA 300A injury summary during posting season (covered employers).
Shared responsibility

Subcontractors & the multi-employer site

Every subcontractor is responsible for their own crew's safety and carries their own General Liability + Workers' Comp (COI). But on a multi-employer site the GC has duties too.

  • All sub crews complete the site orientation before starting.
  • Controlling employer (BuilderK) — general oversight to detect & correct hazards.
  • Creating / exposing / correcting employers each carry their share.
  • COI & W-9 compliance lives under People & partners → Subcontractors.

Per-jobsite safety setup

Stand this up before crews mobilize — and keep it current.
  • Competent person assigned
  • Site orientation ready
  • OSHA poster + emergency numbers posted
  • First-aid kit + eyewash stocked
  • Fire extinguisher on site
  • SDS binder accessible
  • Fall-protection plan if any work > 6 ft
  • Excavation / cave-in plan if digging
  • Silica / dust plan for cutting & grinding
  • PPE stocked on site
  • Toolbox-talk schedule set
  • 911 site address confirmed & posted
